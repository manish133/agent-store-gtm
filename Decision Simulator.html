Model Diagnostics

Statistical analysis of the FY2025 dataset · what we could fit, what we couldn't, and why
Headline finding: The provided FY2025 file appears to be synthetic / uniformly-distributed dummy data. The variables are statistically independent of each other — none of the causal relationships we'd expect in a real QSR (e.g. prep-time → CSAT, training → attrition) are detectable in this data. 25 of 25 hypothesized relationships came back as statistical noise (within-store correlation between −0.03 and +0.03, p > 0.05). Only one real pattern is baked in: the Q4 +30% festival lift.
As a result, the simulator continues to use industry-heuristic coefficients (clearly labeled in the Admin tab), not regressions fit to this file. When real production data is available, the Admin tab's coefficients can be re-tuned directly.

Methodology

How the tests were run

Panel construction. Built a daily-store panel (2,920 rows = 365 days × 8 stores) by merging Sales, Operations, HR, Customer Experience, and Supply Chain sheets on Date + Store.

Hypothesis tests. For each expected causal relationship, computed: (1) raw Pearson correlation, (2) within-store correlation (driver and outcome de-meaned by store) to control for store fixed effects, (3) OLS slope and standard error.

Verdict thresholds. A relationship is "fit-able" only if the within-store |r| exceeds 0.10 with p < 0.01 — a deliberately conservative bar to avoid false discoveries when running 25 simultaneous tests.

Definitional vs causal. Two correlations exceed 0.10 in the raw data (Revenue ↔ AOV: r=+0.90, Orders ↔ AOV: r=−0.42), but both are mathematical identities (Revenue ≡ Orders × AOV) — not causal relationships and not used as model inputs.

Why heuristics still work. The simulator's coefficients come from published QSR industry research (price elasticity, marketing reach, CSAT→repeat conversion). They're directionally correct and roughly right in magnitude — sufficient for what-if exploration and strategic conversation, but not for precise forecasting.
